Project Description

My project is called the Untruth Truth, which tackles the biases and governmental censorship in media reporting on major issues. It will be an online catalog, with 3-5 collections, spanning different relevant issues reported in the last year. Each collection will consist of 3-5 different sources with an analysis of the rhetoric, reporting time, major players, and effect on the global economy or society, with one topic per collection. The goal is to have multiple news sources in multiple nations across the globe, with different news powerhouse owners and/or independent reporters. The purpose of this catalog is to uncover a pattern of media suppression by dissecting the language used to describe the who, what, where, and why of media reporting. This topic piqued my interest in recent days, due to the changing policies within the US, and the responses of others outside of the U.S. In one social media post, on TikTok by @salmajabarique, it was asked, "To all non-americans, what news are you being told that we aren't," which received the most likes (527,700), most comments (7,144), and most saves (33,000) for this question. The amount of traction shown in this post's engagement shows that this topic is prevelant to people in the US who perhaps feel they are not receiving the same full story as those outside of the U.S. In the past, it used to be a shared belief that media suppression only occured in authoritarian governments. This catalog will prove that even with Democracy and the 1st amendment protecting the media, we, the American viewers, are not protected from purposefully misconstrued information. This project will go to show that digital art can make a grandstand, as a form of "Great Refusal", against the suppression of information by making others aware. I believe the first step to resistance and change is awareness and advocacy, which this project entails.
