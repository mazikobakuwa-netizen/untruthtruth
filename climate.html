Climate Change

Climate change is a topic many are not clear on because of how it is relayed from the media, in complex scientific terms rather than laymens terms. In the media, climate changed is acknowledged, without action based approaches being discussed.

The Wire: Interview | ‘Without Justice, Climate Action Will Fail as Fossil Fuel Lock-Ins Deepen’

Source Link
Defines climate change as an issue solved by governance as well as global accountability, including in policy of how companies operate using fossil fuel. Places responsibility on politicians but specifcially naems Pakistan and the Netherlands as examples, showing bias towards who is really responsible for governance, while diminishing accountability for those apart of the Paris Agreement, implying that ambition is not enough without action.

Bloomberg: Wall Street is Turning Climate Finance Into an Energy Security Pitch

Source Link
Describes how investors and wall street are using the reliance AI has on energy into pitches to secure financing in exchange for energy security, rather than prioritizing decarbonization. The carbon footprint may increase for the sake of profit and the ventures into AI, and Wall Street investors are moving to become the funding pioneers. It calls for a focus on environmental pragmatism rather than empathy for the climate (what can the world do for us rather than what we can do for the world.
Bias: Low Favors: Neutral

Copernicus: 2024 is the first year to exceed 1.5°C above pre-industrial level

Source Link
Analyzes how the climate has changed since efforts by the EU and the Paris agreement, implying that they are possible but ambitious compared the rate of change being experienced due to El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO). Notes that the efforts of "ECMWF, NASA, NOAA, the UK Met Office, Berkeley Earth and the World Meteorological Organisation (WMO)" are "concerted" with the same goal to bring real impact based on the data, rather than the governance.
